Terms of use

The deal between you and DrCrop.

Effective 2026-05-22.

The short version

  • DrCrop produces decision-support information — red/yellow/green prescription maps from drone imagery. We are not your PCA.
  • The actual pesticide recommendation is written and signed by a California-licensed PCA, either ours or yours. You and your PCA decide what to spray, when, where, and how much.
  • The free audit is exactly that — free. If we can't show acres to skip on the verification re-fly, you owe us nothing.
  • You own the imagery and prescription maps for blocks you authorize us to fly.
  • We don't warrant agronomic outcomes. Weather, pest pressure, equipment, and grower decisions all affect what happens in the field.

What DrCrop does

DrCrop is software that turns drone orthomosaics of your vineyard into a red/yellow/green prescription map: which acres need treatment, which need scouting, which can be skipped. A California-licensed Pest Control Adviser (PCA) reviews and signs the prescription before it lands in your hands. We can also re-fly the block after your treatment to verify the savings.

What DrCrop is not

DrCrop is not a PCA. DrCrop is not a chemical retailer. DrCrop does not apply pesticides. DrCrop is not a substitute for in-field scouting, your existing agronomic advisors, or your own judgment.

The map we produce is decision-support information. The legally regulated pesticide recommendation — what to apply, at what rate, when — is written by a licensed PCA, signed, and delivered to you. California Food and Agricultural Code requires this; we take it seriously.

The free audit

If we accept your audit request, we will fly one block of your choice (typically 5–40 acres) on a mutually agreed date during a single visit. We will deliver the orthomosaic, a PCA-signed prescription map, and a one-page summary PDF. After your treatment pass we will return for one verification re-fly during the same season.

The audit is free under one condition: we will not invoice you for the work above unless the verification flight shows quantifiable savings against the baseline you described in the signup form. If we cannot show savings, you owe nothing. We reserve the right to define "quantifiable savings" in good faith; we will explain the math in the report.

Post-audit pricing

If you want to continue beyond the free audit, pricing is per-block ($500/block flight) or per-acre ($8–$15/acre/pass) or a 25% shared-savings arrangement against your baseline herbicide spend. Pricing changes only with written agreement.

Your obligations

  • Block access. Provide reasonable access to the block on the agreed flight date.
  • Boundary. Tell us where your block ends and your neighbor's begins. KML, GeoJSON, or a marked-up satellite image all work.
  • Spray history. Be truthful about what you've sprayed and when. Verification math depends on it.
  • Authorization. Confirm you have the legal right to authorize drone flights over the block (you own it, lease it, or have written consent from whoever does).

Our obligations

  • Maintain a current FAA Part 107 certification for the pilot in command.
  • Maintain drone hull, general commercial liability, and professional E&O insurance.
  • Comply with FAA airspace rules (LAANC where applicable).
  • Have the prescription map reviewed and signed by a California-licensed PCA before delivery.
  • Treat your imagery and contact information per our privacy notice.

No warranty on agronomic outcomes

We use our best engineering and agronomic judgment to produce useful maps. We don't and can't guarantee yield, vine health, or any other agronomic outcome. Weather, pest pressure, equipment calibration, application timing, and your own decisions all influence what happens to your crop. The map is a tool; it doesn't replace your judgment or your PCA's judgment.

Liability

To the maximum extent allowed by California law, our total liability to you for any claim relating to DrCrop is limited to the amount you paid us in the prior 12 months (which is $0 for the free audit). We're not liable for indirect, consequential, or incidental damages — lost profits, lost yield, business interruption.

None of this limits liability that California law says cannot be limited (gross negligence, willful misconduct, fraud).

Termination

Either party can end the engagement at any time, in writing. If you terminate before the verification flight, we'll deliver the work-in-progress as-is. If we terminate, we'll refund any unflown pre-paid amount within 14 days.

Governing law

California law governs these terms. Disputes go to the state or federal courts in Sonoma County, California.
