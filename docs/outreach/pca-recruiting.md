# PCA partner — recruiting playbook

The whole funnel is vapor until a California-licensed PCA signs every
prescription map. This is the single highest-leverage relationship in
the company right now. Treat it like hiring a co-founder, not a vendor.

## Why a PCA partner is non-negotiable

- California law (FAC §11401 et seq.): a written pesticide recommendation
  must be made by a licensed PCA. DrCrop's red/yellow/green map is
  "decision-support information," not a recommendation — but the grower
  is going to ask "who signs this?" on the first call.
- Trust: a PCA signature converts a software tool into a credible
  agronomic product. Without it, DrCrop is "some app."
- Distribution: one happy PCA brings 5–20 vineyards. The PCA is the
  fastest channel to volume after the first pilot.

## Who we want

- California PCA license, active, in good standing.
- Already works in Napa / Sonoma / Mendocino / Lake.
- Specialty crop experience (vineyards specifically — not just row crops).
- Comfortable with drone imagery (not a hard requirement; we'll teach).
- Independent or small firm. Big ag retail PCAs have conflicts and slow
  legal review cycles.

Where to find candidates:
- CDPR PCA licensee directory (public lookup by county).
- UC Cooperative Extension viticulture advisors — they know every working PCA.
- LinkedIn search: "PCA" + "vineyard" + "Sonoma OR Napa".
- Sustainable Winegrowing California (SWC) certified properties — their
  consulting PCAs lean low-input.

## The deal we offer (v1 proposal)

> **Review fee:** $150 per block reviewed (audit and post-audit flights).
> Paid on every map signed, whether or not the grower pays us.
>
> **Performance share:** 10% of net DrCrop revenue from any vineyard the
> PCA introduces — paid for the duration of that grower's contract.
>
> **Time commitment:** ~30 minutes per block to review the map, adjust
> zones if needed, and sign. Async — no scheduled calls required.
>
> **Liability:** PCA carries normal professional liability for their
> recommendation. DrCrop carries E&O for the software output.
>
> **Exclusivity:** none — PCA can keep advising the same clients on
> non-DrCrop work.

This is a starting point. Real numbers come out of the first conversation
once you understand their normal billable rate and book size.

## Call agenda (20 minutes)

1. **Their world (5 min).** How many clients, how do they bill, what's
   their biggest time-sink in writing prescriptions today.
2. **Show the artifact (5 min).** Open `drcrop-demo.fly.dev/#sample`,
   generate a synthetic flight, walk through the PDF and KML. Be honest
   that today's map is from synthetic imagery.
3. **The ask (5 min).** "I need a PCA to review and sign 1–10 prescription
   maps a month this season. Here's the proposed deal: $150/block, 10%
   referral share." Pause. Let them push back on numbers.
4. **Liability and process (5 min).** Walk through how they'd see the map
   (email PDF? web link?), what edits they can make, how they sign, who
   delivers the signed version to the grower.

## Objections (and answers)

**"What if I disagree with the map?"**
You override. The signed prescription is whatever you write — the AI map
is just the starting point. We'll log your edits and use them to improve
the model.

**"What's my liability if the grower follows the map and has a yield loss?"**
Same as any prescription you write today. We're decision-support; you're
the licensed professional making the call. DrCrop carries software E&O.
We're not asking you to sign anything riskier than your normal book.

**"Can the AI replace me?"**
No, and that's a feature not a bug. California regs require a PCA. We
need you to sign. We benefit from your judgment. We're not interested
in changing that — we want to make your hour go further.

**"What if I'm too busy?"**
30 minutes per block, async, on your schedule. If we book more volume than
you can handle, we'll bring on a second PCA — you get the referral share
on every block your introductions produce, even after.

## Red flags — do not partner

- Wants exclusive territory before any volume.
- Pushes for an unrealistic per-block fee ($500+) at the pilot stage.
- Won't put time commitments in writing.
- Has a side relationship with a chemical retailer that creates a conflict.
- Negative reviews / disciplinary record on the CDPR license lookup.
