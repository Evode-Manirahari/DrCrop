# Cold outreach templates

Two scripts: one for vineyard managers (Tier 1), one for PCAs (Tier 2).
Customize the first sentence per recipient — never send the same email
twice without one specific line that proves you researched them.

---

## Vineyard manager — first touch

**Subject A (curiosity):** Free spray-waste audit on one [Sonoma/Napa] vineyard block
**Subject B (specific):** Cutting under-vine herbicide acres on [vineyard name]
**Subject C (numbers):** 50% less under-vine herbicide on one of your blocks?

> Hi [first name],
>
> I'm Evode — I run DrCrop, a small software + drone outfit working with NorCal
> vineyards this season.
>
> I noticed [one specific detail: a recent press piece, organic certification,
> a block boundary visible on satellite, a mutual contact]. That's why I'm
> reaching out: under-vine weed pressure varies a lot block-to-block, and
> blanket herbicide passes usually treat 30–60% of acres that don't need it.
>
> I'd like to fly one of your blocks for free. I'll deliver a red/yellow/green
> prescription map, PCA-reviewed, and re-fly after your spray pass to show
> the acres skipped and confirm yield was not hurt. **If we can't show savings,
> you don't pay.** Total time on your end: ~20 minutes.
>
> Worth a 15-minute call this week or next?
>
> Evode Manirahari
> [phone] · [email] · drcrop-demo.fly.dev
>
> *NorCal pilot, 2026 season — 5 vineyard slots, 2 are taken.*

**Notes:**
- The "2 are taken" line is honest only if true. If 0 are taken, drop it or replace with "limited slots".
- Don't attach a deck. Link to a sample report instead — `drcrop-demo.fly.dev/#sample`.
- Send Tue–Thu mornings. Avoid harvest weekends.

### Bump #1 (4 business days later)

> Hi [first name] — bumping this up in case it got buried.
>
> The free-audit ask is open through [date 3 weeks out]. Even if it's a no
> for this season, I'd love 10 minutes to learn how you currently decide
> which blocks get under-vine treatment.
>
> — Evode

### Bump #2 (7 business days later)

> Last one — promise. If I should reach a different contact at [vineyard],
> who's the right person? I'll route through them and stop bothering you.
>
> — Evode

## PCA — first touch

**Subject:** Tool for your under-vine prescriptions — looking for a NorCal PCA partner

> Hi [first name],
>
> I run DrCrop. We turn vineyard drone images into red/yellow/green prescription
> maps so growers can skip the acres that don't need an under-vine pass. The
> piece I'm missing — and the piece that matters — is a licensed PCA who signs
> off on the recommendation.
>
> I'm not building a PCA replacement. I'm building a tool that gives PCAs
> better data and saves them mapping time. The PCA stays in the loop, signs
> the prescription, and gets paid for review on every block we audit.
>
> Open to a 20-minute call to walk through how the partnership could work?
> If you have a [Sonoma/Napa] block you're already advising on, I'll fly it
> for free as part of the conversation.
>
> Evode Manirahari · [phone] · [email]

## What to track per outreach

In `icp.md`, update the Status column. Also log in a personal scratch file
(not in the repo):
- date sent
- which subject line / template variant
- their reply (or silence)
- what you learned about objections
- whether you got a meeting

After 20 sends, review: which subject got the most opens, which line in
the body people replied to, which county converts. Adjust before the next 20.
